About Me

I am a VR developer based in Ottawa, Canada.

My undergraduate background is in Earth Sciences from Carleton University and I am a graduate from the VR online degree program at Udacity.

Before jumping into VR, I developed desktop and mobile games with Unity with the help of tons of YouTube videos and online courses. I have spent the past year building applications and games for Oculus Rift, HTC Vive, Gear VR, and Google Cardboard.

Now, I develop brilliant VR experiences and games with the power of iteration, user testing, and some good old-fashioned research. I learn by developing and contributing to open source VR and cross-platform games, including Puzzler, Tetro, and MazeVR.

n my free time, I write about my developer experience and find ways to assist other developers on their software journey. You can also find me either going back to old projects and constantly tweaking or just reading a good book.

Puzzler

As part of my coursework in the VR nanodegree at Udacity, I designed, tested and iterated a mobile VR game called Puzzler. In this game, the player is to enter a dreary room and test their memorization skills by trying to replicate patterns shown to them. The player can leave the room and beat the game after correctly replicating the patterns.
